## 1. GROW Model (Goal, Reality, Options, Will)

**What:** 4-step coaching conversation framework by Sir John Whitmore. Most widely used coaching model globally due to its simplicity.

**App Content:**
- **4-Screen Guided Workflow:** Each screen = one GROW step with specific prompts
- **Goal Setting Templates:** SMART goal creation with guided fields
- **Reality Check Assessments:** Honest self-assessment of current situation, resources, obstacles
- **Options Brainstorm Tool:** List 5+ approaches, narrow to top 2-3
- **Commitment Contract:** Specific next actions, deadlines, accountability

**Example Exercise — "GROW Worksheet":**
1. **G - Goal:** "What do you want to achieve in the next 90 days?"
2. **R - Reality:** "Where are you now? What have you already tried? What's in your way?"
3. **O - Options:** "What could you do? List at least 5 possibilities, even wild ones."
4. **W - Will:** "Which option(s) will you commit to? By when? What's your first step this week?"

## 2. CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Techniques)

**What:** Structured approach to identifying and changing negative thought patterns. Adapted as "Cognitive Behavioral Coaching" (CBC) for non-clinical contexts.

**App Content:**
- **Thought Records:** 7-step structured exercise (situation → thought → emotion → evidence → balanced thought → new rating)
- **Cognitive Distortion Checklists:** Identify patterns like all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, mind-reading
- **Behavioral Experiments:** Small action items to test negative predictions
- **Worry Journals:** Sort concerns into "practical" (actionable) vs. "hypothetical" (no fix)

**Example Exercise — "Thought Record":**
1. Situation: "My manager didn't reply to my email for 2 days"
2. Automatic thought: "She thinks my work is bad and is ignoring me"
3. Emotion: Anxiety (8/10)
4. Evidence for: "She usually replies quickly"
5. Evidence against: "She mentioned she has back-to-back meetings this week"
6. Balanced thought: "She's likely busy. If I don't hear back by tomorrow, I can follow up."
7. New emotion rating: Anxiety (3/10)

**Research:** App-based CBT (Mind Booster Green) was effective in an RCT published in JMIR mHealth. Noom's entire curriculum draws from CBT.

## 3. ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy)

**What:** Focus on accepting difficult thoughts/feelings while committing to values-aligned actions. Six core processes: cognitive defusion, acceptance, present moment awareness, self-as-context, values clarification, committed action.

**App Content:**
- **Values Clarification Exercises:** Identify and rank core values across life domains
- **Cognitive Defusion:** "I'm having the thought that..." technique
- **Values-Based Action Planning:** Create specific actions tied to stated values
- **Willingness Scale:** Rate willingness to experience discomfort in service of values
- **The Choice Point:** Visual tool showing "toward values" vs. "away from discomfort"
- **Values Bull's-Eye:** Dartboard diagram with 4 quadrants showing value alignment

## 4. Positive Psychology (PERMA)

**What:** Seligman's five pillars of wellbeing: Positive Emotion, Engagement (flow), Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment.

**App Content:**
- **PERMA Assessment:** Rate on each pillar, repeat periodically to track growth
- **Three Good Things Journal:** Write 3 things that went well and why (each evening)
- **Strengths Spotting:** Identify top character strengths, exercises to use them in new ways
- **Gratitude Letters:** Guided writing to express appreciation
- **Flow Audit:** Track activities and rate engagement level

---

## 5. SFBC (Solution-Focused Brief Coaching)

**What:** Focuses on solutions rather than problems. Directs attention to what's already working and what small steps can move toward the desired future.

**App Content:**
- **Miracle Question:** "If you woke up tomorrow and your problem was solved, what's different?"
- **Scaling Questions:** "On a scale of 1-10, where are you now? What would +1 look like?"
- **Exception Finding:** "When was this problem less severe? What was different?"
- **Best Hopes Exercise:** Articulate what you hope to get from each module

---

## 6. Motivational Interviewing (MI)

**What:** Empirically supported method to resolve ambivalence about change. Five principles: express empathy, develop discrepancy, roll with resistance, support self-efficacy, enhance intrinsic motivation.

**App Content:**
- **Decisional Balance Exercises:** 2x2 grid — pros/cons of changing vs. staying the same
- **Readiness Rulers:** 1-10 scale on importance + confidence, with follow-up prompts
- **Values-Behavior Gap Analysis:** Rate how aligned current behavior is with each value
- **Autonomy-Supportive Language:** All app copy offers choices, not prescriptions

---

## 7. Gottman Method (Relationships)

**What:** 40+ years of relationship research. Structured exercises for communication and connection.

**App Content:**
- **Love Maps:** Learning your partner's inner world
- **Four Horsemen Checklist:** Identify destructive patterns (criticism, contempt, defensiveness, stonewalling)
- **Bids for Connection Tracker:** Notice and log bids given/received daily
- **Communication Scripts:** "I Statement" templates, active listening practice
- **Conflict Debrief Worksheet:** What happened, what I felt, what I needed, what I'll try next

---

## Supporting Frameworks

**Self-Determination Theory (SDT):** Three fundamental needs — Autonomy, Competence, Relatedness. Design principle: offer choices, provide progress feedback, incorporate community.

**Transtheoretical Model (Stages of Change):** Users move through Precontemplation → Contemplation → Preparation → Action → Maintenance. Serve appropriate content per stage.
